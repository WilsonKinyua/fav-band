Who are Sauti Sol?

Bien-Aimé Baraza, Willis Chimano, and Savara Mudigi created the Kenyan afro-pop band Sauti Sol in Nairobi, Kenya in 2005. Polycarp Otieno, a guitarist, joined the group before they changed their name to Sauti Sol.

Mwanzo, Sauti Sol's debut studio album, was released on November 1, 2008, to critical acclaim. Sol Filosofia, their second studio album, was released on February 25, 2011, and received numerous honors and nominations. The band collaborated with South African rapper and song producer Spoek Mathambo to create a self-titled extended play on June 18, 2012.

The band has completed several successful tours in Africa and Europe, topped Kenyan charts, and gained international attention with shows in Europe and the United States, as well as television appearances and numerous accolades and nominations, including a concert performance in Kenya in 2011 with Ladysmith Black Mambazo, a South African a cappella group.

Why the Sauti Sol?

For the following reasons, I would always choose them over any other band:

  • Musical Style - Their musical style has been described as abstract, African-pop, urban-colored vintage, uniquely hilarious.
  • Their Inspiration - Sauti Sol's music is highly influenced by everyday life.
  • Fusion of Poetry and Music - Various Kenyan Poets that have explored other kinds of poetry performance not with the same success as Sauti Sol.

SAUTI SOL MEMBERS

Willis Austin Chimano

Willis Austin Chimano is a member of the popular all-boy band Sauti Sol and is a vocalist, keytarist, and saxophonist.

Savara Mudigi

Savara Mudigi is a Grammy Award - winning Singer, Songwriter, Music producer at Sauti Sol.

Polycarp Otieno

Polycarp Otieno is a famous Guitarist. Kenyan guitarist and producer of the popular music group Sauti Sol who are best known for albums such as Mwanzo and Sol Filosofia.

Bien-Aimé Baraza

Bien-Aimé Baraza is songwriter, Guitar, Piano and also a member of Sauti Sol.

Top Songs or albums

The following is a list of their songs or album:

  • Sura Yako - “Sura Yako (Your Face)” is the lead track on Live and Die in Afrika. Produced by Sauti Sol alongside Cedric "Cedo" Kadenyi, the single blends intricate guitar lines with a bumping beat and vocal harmonies from the group to make for an undeniable ear worm.
  • Lazizi - “Lazizi” was the first single from Sauti Sol’s debut album Mwanzo. A more laid-back and serene song, it follows the story of a workingman pleading to get a girl’s number so he can take her out on a date.
  • Kuliko Jana - Sauti Sol returned to their old high school, Upper Hill School, in Nairobi to record the music video for “Kuliko Jana,” a standout single from Live and Die in Afrika that is quickly growing into a global hit.
  • Blue Uniform - The second single from their debut album, “Blue Uniform" sees Sauti Sol addressing the issue of police harassment against Kenya’s youth.
  • Nishike - Sauti Sol’s “Nishike” (Swahili for “Touch Me”) is a slow jam for late night playlists everywhere built on undulating synth chords and smooth vocals.
  • Soma Kijana - "Soma Kijana" was released as the lead single from Sauti Sol’s sophomore album Sol Filosofia. The song was inspired by the late Kenyan Benga singer Daudi Kabaka, who the group cite as one of their biggest influences.
  • Unconditionally Bae - "Sauti Sol connected with Tanzanian star Alikiba for “Unconditionally Bae,” a dance floor track about how hard it is to find love in the modern world.
  • Coming Home - “Coming Home,” a quiet and melancholic acoustic guitar ballad, was released as the second single from Sol Filosofia.
  • Shake Yo Bam Bam - “Shake Yo Bam Bam” is Sauti Sol's party track. The single, which is built on a bouncing bass line, was written in the mindset of 1990s Kenyan music styles. The track’s Nairobi-shot music video was directed by famed Nigerian director Clarence Peters.
